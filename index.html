What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperature, precipitation, and other atmospheric patterns. It’s primarily driven by human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes.

Impact of Climate Change

Rising Temperatures

Global temperatures have risen by approximately 1.2°C since the late 19th century, leading to heatwaves, droughts, and severe weather events.

Melting Ice Caps

The Arctic is warming at twice the global rate. Melting ice caps threaten to flood coastal cities and ecosystems.

Extreme Weather

Climate change increases the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events such as hurricanes, floods, and wildfires.

Actions to Combat Climate Change

  • Reduce energy consumption by using renewable sources like wind and solar energy.
  • Shift to plant-based diets to reduce methane emissions from livestock.
  • Support green policies, eco-friendly transportation, and waste reduction.
